# Zizo Circle — Qatar Go-To-Market Plan

> Built using the **launch-strategy** skill (ORB framework + 5-phase launch). Read alongside `brand-identity-qatar.md` and `.agents/product-marketing-context.md`.

---

## Executive summary

We are launching Zizo Circle into a Qatar market that has high smartphone penetration, high disposable income, an 88% expat population with chronic friend-group churn, and **no entrenched local competitor** in interest-matched social discovery + venue cashback. The plan is a **5-phase, 90-day launch** anchored in Doha's three highest-density social districts (Msheireb, Lusail Boulevard, The Pearl), with **owned channels at the core** (WhatsApp Communities, email, in-app), **rented channels for reach** (Instagram, TikTok, Snapchat — in that order for Qatar), and **borrowed channels for credibility** (5–8 hand-picked Doha-based creators across English, Arabic, Filipino, and Hindi audiences).

**90-day target**: 15,000 verified signups, 4,500 weekly actives, 12 venue partners live with cashback, 1 referral loop deployed.

## 1. Market context (Qatar-specific)

### What's true about marketing in Qatar
- **Population:** ~3.0M residents; ~88% non-Qatari. Largest expat groups: Indian, Bangladeshi, Nepali, Egyptian, Filipino, Sri Lankan, Pakistani, Sudanese, with Western and Levantine communities also significant.
- **Languages:** English is the lingua franca of work and most digital surfaces. Arabic for nationals and government. Hindi/Urdu, Tagalog, Malayalam serve large communities. **Ship English-first; add Arabic in phase 4; add Tagalog + Hindi creator content from phase 3.**
- **Dominant platforms (resident usage, ranked):** WhatsApp (universal) > Instagram > Snapchat > TikTok > X/Twitter > LinkedIn > Facebook (declining among under-35s).
- **Snapchat is bigger here than most non-GCC markets realize.** Snap Map and Discover stories are major venue-discovery surfaces, especially for Qatari nationals and Khaleeji users.
- **TikTok is fast-growing** but skews younger and less Qatari-national.
- **Influencer marketing** is the dominant paid acquisition channel in the GCC, not paid search.
- **Search behavior** is highly Instagram-driven (people search for venues *on* Instagram, not Google) but Google still drives the long-tail "X in Doha" intent.
- **Payments:** Apple Pay, Google Pay, and local cards (NAPS) are universal. Cashback is well understood (Talabat Pro, Snoonu, Carrefour MyCLUB).

### High-traffic digital spaces in Qatar
| Surface | Why it matters | How we use it |
|---|---|---|
| Instagram (Reels + Stories + Geotagged grid posts) | #1 venue discovery surface in Qatar | Daily Reels; geotag every post; partner-venue takeovers |
| Snapchat (Snap Map + Discover) | Huge among Qatari nationals & Khaleeji 18–28 | Geofilters at partner venues; sponsored lenses for launch |
| WhatsApp Communities | Where actual friend-groups live | Branded community for early access; venue-night announcements |
| TikTok | Fastest-growing for under-25 expats | Padel-trick clips, "things to do in Doha" lists, Filipino + Indian creator partnerships |
| Talabat / Snoonu in-app placements | Captive food-delivery audience | Co-branded promo with cashback partners |
| Doha-news Instagram accounts | @ilovedoha, @thepeninsulaqatar, @qatarliving etc. | Sponsored stories, founder interviews |
| Qatar Living forums | Still high-traffic for expats | Native posts in "Welcome to Qatar" + "Things to Do" sections |
| Education City student WhatsApp groups | Concentrated 18–24 audience | Campus ambassadors |
| Padel Qatar / Aspire Zone Instagram | Hobbyist concentration | Co-marketing on padel + fitness content |

## 3. Five-phase launch (90 days)

### Phase 1 — Internal Launch (Days 1–14, "Friends & Family")
**Goal:** Validate end-to-end flow with friendly Doha-resident users; uncover Qatar-specific bugs (Arabic input, RTL edge cases, +974 OTP delivery, Snap-share intents).

**Actions:**
- Recruit 30 testers in person — mix of nationalities, ages 22–35, half new-to-Doha
- WhatsApp group for daily feedback
- Run 6 onboarding sessions in person (3 at Msheireb café, 3 on Education City campus)
- Stand up basic landing page with email waitlist (`zizocircle.qa/early`)
- Confirm cashback flow with 2 willing café owners in Msheireb (paper-prototype QR if needed)

**Exit criteria:** ≥ 25/30 testers complete profile and one match attempt; OTP delivery works on Ooredoo + Vodafone Qatar; one cashback redemption end-to-end.

### Phase 2 — Alpha (Days 15–35, "First 500")
**Goal:** First external validation; build a credible waitlist.

**Actions:**
- Open invite-only access via WhatsApp Community "Zizo Doha Circle"
- Launch waitlist on `zizocircle.qa` with **referral mechanic** (tiered: invite 3 = early access, invite 10 = first month QAR 50 wallet credit)
- Soft-launch Instagram + Snapchat accounts; 3 posts/week each
- Brief 2 Doha lifestyle creators for paid posts in week 4
- Sign 5 venue launch partners with signed term sheets

**Exit criteria:** 500+ waitlist signups; 70%+ profile completion among activated alpha users; 5 venue partners live.

### Phase 3 — Beta (Days 36–60, "Public Beta")
**Goal:** Generate buzz, refine product with broader feedback, build venue density.

**Actions:**
- Open public beta: anyone with a Qatari phone number can join (still labeled "Beta")
- "Beta" badge in app dashboard; in-app feedback button
- 8 creator partnerships activated (mix of English / Arabic / Tagalog / Hindi creators)
- Launch programmatic SEO landing pages (see `programmatic-seo-qatar.md`)
- Add cashback partners to 12 total
- First "Zizo Night" in-person events at 2 partner venues — invite top 80 active users; QAR 100 wallet credit just for showing up
- Tagalog + Hindi creator content begins

**Exit criteria:** 5,000 signups; 1,200 weekly actives; press coverage in at least 2 Qatar-based outlets (Doha News, ILoveQatar, Marhaba, Qatar Tribune, etc.); 1 university ambassador event held.

### Phase 4 — Early Access (Days 61–80, "Scale")
**Goal:** Validate at scale; localize Arabic; prepare full launch.

**Actions:**
- Ship Arabic translation (in-app + landing pages); native QA pass
- Launch Apple Sign-In + Google Sign-In (close the "coming soon" gap — currently friction)
- Launch in-app referral mechanic (giveable QAR 50 wallet credit)
- Run first paid campaigns: Instagram + Snapchat geo-targeted to Doha + Lusail + Al Wakrah, age 21–38, interest targeting (food, sports, music)
- "Zizo Night" weekly events at rotating partner venues
- Ambassador program live at all 6 Education City + Qatar University campuses

**Exit criteria:** 12,000 signups; 3,500 WAU; CAC validated under QAR 35; cashback redemption rate > 25% of WAU.

### Phase 5 — Full Launch (Days 81–90, "Public Launch")
**Goal:** Open the floodgates; capture first wave of organic word-of-mouth.

**Launch day touchpoints:**
- App Store + Play Store featured submissions (App Store editorial pitched 2 weeks earlier via App Store Connect)
- Founder-led press: 3 outlets (Doha News, The Peninsula, Qatar Tribune) + 1 Khaleej Times piece for regional reach
- Product Hunt launch (Tuesday, 12:01 AM PT — accept that PH skews tech-Western; the credibility / backlinks are still worth it)
- All-creator coordinated post burst on launch day (8+ creators)
- Snapchat sponsored geofilter live in Msheireb, Lusail, Pearl
- Instagram Reels paid push: QAR 25,000 budget across launch week
- WhatsApp Community blast to 10,000+ waitlist
- In-venue: window clings activated at all 12 partner venues
- Email blast to entire waitlist with 48-hour "first 1,000 signups get QAR 100 wallet credit"

**Exit criteria (Day 90):** 15,000 signups, 4,500 WAU, 12 venue partners live, NPS ≥ 45, one referral loop measurably contributing 15%+ of signups.

## 6. Risk register

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| OTP delivery fails on Vodafone Qatar | Medium | Test with both Ooredoo & Vodafone in phase 1; have email-OTP fallback ready (already in code) |
| Venue partners back out before launch | Medium | Sign 12 to land 8; use signed term sheets, not handshakes |
| Cultural misstep in copy / imagery | Low-Medium | Native Arabic + Khaleeji review on all public copy; brand-safety checklist enforced (see `brand-identity-qatar.md` §7) |
| App is mistaken for a dating app | Medium-High | Lead all creator briefs and press with "platonic / interest-matched / group-first" framing; never use heart icons in primary brand surfaces |
| Cashback economics break | Medium | Cap monthly cashback per user at QAR 200; venue funds 50% of cashback in partner agreement |
| Ramadan timing mishandled | High if ignored | Hard-coded prayer-time calendar from day 1; phase 4 launch landing in Ramadan is acceptable IF the iftar campaign is the headline |
| Qatari national audience doesn't adopt | Medium | Snap-first creator strategy + 2 Qatari creator partnerships in beta phase |
