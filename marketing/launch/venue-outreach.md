# Zizo Circle — Cold Outreach to Qatar Venues

> Audience: owners and managers of cafés, padel courts, casual restaurants, and board-game/social spots across Doha, Lusail, the Pearl, Msheireb, and Education City.
> Channel mix in Qatar: **WhatsApp gets replies; email proves you're real.** Send both.
> Voice: peer-to-peer, low-key confident, lead with what they get. No "synergy," no "leverage," no "best-in-class."
> One ask per message: a 15-min meeting at their venue, you bring coffee.

---

## A — Email v1 (cold, first touch)

**Subject:** quick question about [Venue Name]

> Hi [First Name] —
>
> I run Zizo Circle, a new app in Qatar that matches people on shared interests (padel, brunch, board games, that kind of thing) and pays them cashback when they go meet up. Beta opens this month across Doha and Lusail.
>
> We're picking 12 launch-cohort venues. [Venue Name] keeps coming up when our beta users describe where they actually want to go in [neighborhood — e.g. Msheireb / Lusail Boulevard / the Pearl] — which is why I'm writing.
>
> The deal is simple:
> — You set the cashback rate (most partners go 8–12%).
> — You only pay on real visits we drive — no listing fee, no monthly cost, no impressions billing.
> — You get a free dashboard showing the customers we send: interest profile, repeat-visit rate, group sizes.
>
> Worth a 15-minute chat at the venue this week? I'll bring coffee — and the one-pager so you can show your team.
>
> [First Name]
> Founder, Zizo Circle
> +974 5xxx xxxx · zizocircle.com

**Why this works (annotations):**
- Subject is internal-looking, lowercase, 4 words — looks like a colleague, not a pitch.
- Opens with what we are (one sentence) so they don't have to guess. Mid-level managers in Qatar tend to read the first 3 lines on mobile and decide.
- Personalisation in line 3 ("keeps coming up... in [neighborhood]") — replace with something real you've heard or noticed; if you can't, use "We're prioritizing [neighborhood] for our launch cohort."
- The deal block is three lines because that's the whole pitch — no jargon, no "leverage."
- One ask, low friction: 15 minutes, at their venue, you bring coffee. The coffee detail signals respect for their time and matches Qatar hospitality norms.

## B — Follow-up #1 (4 days later, if no reply)

**Subject:** re: quick question about [Venue Name]

> Hi [First Name] —
>
> One follow-up — wanted to put a real number to the case.
>
> Our beta users average 2.4 venue visits per month. If 30 of them flow to [Venue Name] in launch month, that's 70+ paying customers showing up because they matched on the app. You only owe cashback on actual visits — so worst case for you is "we sent nobody, you paid nothing."
>
> Even 15 minutes this week at the venue would be enough to walk through it. I'm in [neighborhood] Tuesday and Wednesday afternoon.
>
> [First Name]

**Why this works:**
- New angle: a specific number, not a recycled "just checking in."
- Frames the worst case in their favor — kills the implicit objection that they'll get burned.
- Specific times = easier to say yes.

## C — Follow-up #2 / breakup (10 days after follow-up #1)

**Subject:** closing the loop

> Hi [First Name] —
>
> I'll stop bothering you on this one — I know the inbox is a nightmare.
>
> If [Venue Name] does want a slot in the launch cohort later, we'll be locking the second wave in [month]. You can reach me directly any time at +974 5xxx xxxx.
>
> Either way — best of luck with [observable thing about their venue, e.g. "the new weekend brunch menu"].
>
> [First Name]

**Why this works:**
- Honors the "breakup" — no fake re-engagement next month.
- Leaves a phone number so they can come to you if the timing was just bad.
- Specific closing signal proves you actually went to / followed the venue.

> Hi [First Name] — I'm [Your Name], founder of Zizo Circle. We're a new app launching in Qatar this month that matches people on shared interests (padel, brunch, board games) and pays them cashback when they go meet up.
>
> We're picking 12 launch-cohort venues. [Venue Name] is on our shortlist for [neighborhood].
>
> The short version:
> · You set the cashback rate (most partners go 8–12%)
> · You only pay on real visits we send you — no listing fee, no monthly cost
> · Free dashboard: interest profile, repeat-visit rate, group sizes
>
> Up for a 15-min chat at the venue this week? I'll bring coffee.
>
> Happy to send the one-pager too if that's easier first.

**Why this works:**
- WhatsApp messages in Qatar get read in minutes; structure with line breaks + dot bullets is mobile-native.
- "I'll bring coffee" reads warmer on WhatsApp than email — matches the channel.
- Soft second option ("send the one-pager first") for managers who don't take meetings without paperwork.

## E — WhatsApp follow-up (3 days, no reply)

> Hey [First Name] — one nudge on this. I'm in [neighborhood] Wednesday afternoon if you have 15 minutes between [time]–[time]. If not, totally fair — just tell me and I won't keep nudging 🙏

**Why this works:**
- One-message follow-up. Gives a specific time + an explicit out. People in Qatar appreciate the directness; spammy follow-ups poison the brand fast.

## F — DM via Instagram (when no email/phone available)

> Hey — I really like what you've built at [Venue Name]. I'm [Your Name], founder of Zizo Circle, a new app launching in Qatar. We match people on shared interests and pay them cashback when they go meet up — and we're shortlisting 12 venues for our launch cohort.
>
> Could I get the right person to send a one-page brief to? Happy to drop in this week with coffee.

**Why this works:**
- Social DMs in Qatar are a legitimate channel — many F&B accounts are managed directly by owners.
- "Right person to send a one-page brief to" lowers the social cost of replying — easier than agreeing to a meeting cold.

## Targeting list (suggested first 30 venues to hit)

Group by district. Don't fire all 30 in the same week — start with the 10 that match your highest-interest user clusters (padel + brunch + specialty coffee).

| District | Venue type fit | Examples to research |
|---|---|---|
| Msheireb Downtown | Specialty coffee, casual brunch, co-working café | Roasters, modern Qatari cafés, boutique breakfast spots |
| Lusail Boulevard / Marina | Casual dining, dessert spots, board-game cafés | New-build casual restaurants, dessert chains, game cafés |
| The Pearl / Porto Arabia | Brunch, padel, healthy bowls | Brunch staples, padel clubs, juice/açaí bars |
| Aspire Zone | Padel courts, fitness cafés, healthy meal prep | Padel clubs, post-workout cafés, sports halls |
| Education City | Student-density cafés, casual eats, study spots | Cafés near GU-Q, HBKU, NU-Q campuses |
| Souq Waqif / Old Doha Port | Karak cafés, shisha-free social spots, board games | Karak chains, traditional cafés (afternoon-friendly) |

> Avoid in your first cohort: hotel F&B (slow procurement), licensed venues (off-brand for Zizo), and chains that need GCC HQ approval (long cycle). Independent operators, district managers of small chains, and franchise owners are the fastest 'yes'.

## Tracking sheet (lightweight)

Recommended: Google Sheet with columns:
`Venue · District · Contact name · Channel sent · Date sent · Reply (Y/N/Maybe) · Meeting booked (date) · Term sheet status · Live date · First Zizo visit logged`

Review every Friday afternoon for 15 minutes. Re-prioritize the next week's outreach off this. Don't spray and pray — pace 5–7 outreach attempts per day so you can actually follow through with each one.

---

## What NOT to do (saves you from killing the brand)

- ❌ Bulk-import emails into Mailchimp and blast — Qatar's hospitality scene is small, word travels in days, and you'll be marked as spam.
- ❌ Open with "I hope this email finds you well" or "My name is X and I work at Y."
- ❌ Send a deck before a conversation. The one-pager is the deck.
- ❌ Promise specific user numbers you can't guarantee in writing.
- ❌ Negotiate cashback rates over WhatsApp. That's a meeting conversation.
- ❌ Hand the outreach off to a junior / intern in the first 30 venues. Founder-led closes 3× harder in Qatar's relationship economy.
